I came into this field with passion and hope; I really did. But now that I'm
in, I understand why it's been fearmongered for so long.

It's not because it's hard. I know, Mr Smart-Ass over here. But personally, it
has been very straight-forward. Do the work, read the reference texts, get good
grades.

In essence, there are 3 reasons why I think my physics career is doomed.

### The Gatekeepers

The number of professors that _deliberately_ create their mess they have and do
the bare minimum, **just** so they can "weed out" the unfit, is completely
unacceptable.

Things like "You need to understand Calculus 3 to understand Laser Physics," is
absurd--No, you do not! (It depends on how deep you want to understand it) Yes,
I think that it is beneficial, and that you should understand Calculus 3
anyway, but making students feel like they're missing out on a lot just because
they couldn't keep up with some small equations; it's incredibly hurtful to the
field.

Now why would this be the end of my career? As long as there are these types of
'gatekeepers', I feel as though my entry to any sort of progress is heavily
dampened.

### The Inaccessiblity to Research

Platforms like [ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com) and [Nature
Journal](https://www.nature.com) who put published papers behind paywalls
_seem_ like they are helping to sustain research progress by allegedly
providing funding for the research.

However, it is widely known that these very platforms don't return funding to
the people, but rather it is the scientists that are the ones expected to pay
for their papers to be published, simply because the platforms have the
reputation of a good journal. If you aren't backed by faculty, research is
simply unsustainable.

This _will_ be the death of my career in physics, unless I somehow get a
faculty position.

### The Oversaturated Market

Speaking of faculty, it's obvious that the field is oversaturated. Too many
bright people trying too hard to fill in one slot. I just don't think it's fair
to think that my chances of being the "one" is anything but comedic. As much as
it hurts to say, I'm not special; and neither are the hundreds that are trying.
At this point, the only way that position is filled is by nepotism (which
happens in academia more often than you think).

I'm lucky enough to be born somewhat adjacent to the academic field (which has
immensely helped in understanding the roundabouts), but even then, to think I
can compete with the daughter of the cousin of the deputy dean is absurd.

It's clear I need a back up plan.

## Plan B

Now, I can't confidently say that these alternatives are gonna work out the way
I expect, if at all. Nevertheless, these are just options that span my personal
interests. Think of them as freelancing gigs.

### Web App Development

I'm fairly interested in the web development sphere; I made a few sites for a
few projects of my own. This site was made with [NextJS](https://nextjs.org)
and other technologies including TailwindCSS and ContentLayer (not anymore
since July 2024). I've also made [LectCheck](https://lectcheck.vercel.app)
which implements Prisma and NextAuth and it's been a pretty smooth sailing so
far.

### Graphic Design

I've done some graphic design work for some cocuricular activity before, and I
thoroughly enjoyed it. I made some eye-catching logos and posters that I wish
could be more than just a small idea. Unfortunately, the project was killed
before the pieces were public, so in the vault they stay (though, I might just
host them here). I really think, if given the chance, this type of work would
be very fun and profitable.

## Conclusion

Does this post mean that I'm going to leave my degree and physics altogether?
Hell no. These are just my thoughts on the current state of things. I'm still
going to do my best and try to get the best grades (try to anyway). If
something great happens, great! I'll look forward to expanding my career in
research and teaching(?).

On the contrary, if something bad happens (in which case, I called it), then
I'll be happy to explore other opportunities. Either way, I'll continue to do
what I do best, whatever that is.
