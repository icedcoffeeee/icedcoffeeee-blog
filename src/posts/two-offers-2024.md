---
title: The Two (Opportunities) That Got Away
date: '2024-02-09'
tags:
  - rant
---

So far in my degree, I've been approached by two lecturers to collaborate
and/or work with.

One of them was a teaching assistant position. His teaching was a bit point-
blank, generally speaking. It had no depth and very much algorithmic. So
naturally, I offered to teach extra classes to the other students, as part of
another course (volunteerism), to help lessons be more intuitive; and _guided_,
if you will. It was supposed to show _why_ formulas are the way they are,
rather than brute force. After the semester, I was excited to hear his offer to
monetarily compensate in continuing this effort in upcoming semesters.

The other lecturer had a more “research focused” offer. He was aware I had
experience with Python, especially in library maintenance. I had worked with
him before in a smaller project revolving open source software and the
scientific community (also as part of another course), and one of my opening
lines in proposing his advisory was "I’m a maintainer of manim" lol (I rarely
help maintain the repo these days).

He taught his course _really_ well, like, pedagogically. We could understand
how and why, and his questions nicely led from one idea to another.
Unfortunately, you can imagine my surprise when he graded me a B+ _smh_.
Anyway, sometime during the semester, he asked to meet and offered me to assist
in one of his upcoming projects.

The work was mainly reading documentation of an obscure c++ library and
formulating a working script to be used in the research. Nevertheless, I was
thrilled to hear this! Naively, I was already thinking of the other
opportunities this could lead to (I mean, what else was there to expect as an
undergrad).

At the end of the semester I was left with two choices. And I chose the second
option. I emailed him to express interest, to learn more about this line of
prospect—only to be met with silence. To date, not a peep. Granted, I didn’t
email any further to avoiding spamming, but it definitely could be that he
missed the email. I was still devastated though.

It turns out he was flying abroad for other research stuff I wasn’t aware of,
so maybe I still have a chance. I might hit up the first offer again this new
semester, if he remembers me (I heard it’s common for professors to forget what
an undergrad has done). It's just funny to me in retrospect, I had two
opportunities and blew both of them. I hope something new comes along in the
meantime.
